## STYLE CATEGORIES

### CLEAN / MINIMAL
When to use: SaaS products, consultancies, thought leadership, modern B2B brands.
Prompt descriptors:
minimalist composition, generous negative space, Swiss design aesthetic, clean grid layout, restrained color palette, no visual clutter, flat minimal design, crisp edges, airy and breathable layout, Dieter Rams design philosophy, functional beauty, precision and order

### BOLD / EDITORIAL
When to use: Agency brands, marketing content, thought leadership from contrarian voices, disruptive brands.
Prompt descriptors:
editorial photography, magazine cover composition, high contrast lighting, dynamic diagonal composition, bold graphic design, strong visual hierarchy, Wired magazine aesthetic, dramatic close-up cropping, confident directional light, graphic impact

### CORPORATE / PROFESSIONAL
When to use: Enterprise software, financial services, legal, HR, traditional B2B.
Prompt descriptors:
professional corporate photography, executive portrait aesthetic, clean office environment, business formal, trustworthy and authoritative, neutral color-graded, classic rule-of-thirds composition, credibility-focused visual language, polished and refined

### DARK / PREMIUM
When to use: Luxury B2B, cybersecurity, fintech, premium SaaS, exclusive communities.
Prompt descriptors:
dark background, dramatic studio lighting, high contrast, color accent spotlight, premium dark mode aesthetic, luxurious depth, cinematic lighting, deep shadows with luminous highlights, exclusive and sophisticated, neon accent on dark substrate, Vercel / Linear design aesthetic

### WARM / HUMAN
When to use: People-focused brands, coaching/consulting, HR tech, culture-first companies.
Prompt descriptors:
warm natural lighting, authentic human moments, lifestyle photography, candid composition, genuine expressions, golden hour warmth, earthy color palette, human-centered, relatable and approachable, documentary-style photography, warm film grain aesthetic

### TECH / FUTURISTIC
When to use: AI, deep tech, developer tools, cybersecurity, data companies.
Prompt descriptors:
futuristic tech aesthetic, glowing neon data streams, holographic interface overlay, dark cyberpunk-adjacent professional look, isometric 3D illustration, abstract neural network visualization, cool blue and electric teal palette, high-tech precision, digital grid patterns, particle effects and data flow visualization

### VIBRANT / ENERGETIC
When to use: Marketing agencies, growth-stage startups, event promotion.
Prompt descriptors:
vibrant saturated colors, energetic composition, bold gradient overlays, high-energy visual impact, dynamic motion blur suggestion, maximalist color confidence, attention-grabbing contrast, electric and alive, punchy and memorable

### ILLUSTRATED / ABSTRACT
When to use: Conceptual topics, brand illustration sets, explainer content.
Prompt descriptors:
flat vector illustration, editorial illustration, conceptual metaphor, isometric illustration, geometric abstract composition, Economist magazine illustration style, abstract business concept, stylized figures, hand-crafted warmth, thoughtful visual metaphor

## LIGHTING PRESETS

Golden Hour: warm golden hour sunlight from the left, long soft shadows
Best for: Warm/human brands

Studio Softbox: large softbox lighting from upper left, soft fill from right, no hard shadows
Best for: Clean corporate

Rim / Edge Light: dramatic rim light from behind in teal, subject silhouetted against dark background
Best for: Dark/premium

Rembrandt: Rembrandt lighting, triangular shadow on cheek, single key light from 45 degrees
Best for: Editorial portraits

Window Light: soft diffused natural window light from the right, ambient fill, airy and clean
Best for: Warm/minimal

Cinematic: cinematic three-point lighting, key from upper left, teal fill from right, warm backlight
Best for: Bold/editorial

Neon / Accent: dark environment with single colored LED accent light in electric blue, dramatic contrast
Best for: Tech/futuristic

Backlit: strong backlight creating glow around subject's edges, lens flare suggestion, ethereal
Best for: Aspirational/emotional

## COMPOSITION FRAMEWORKS

For images with TEXT OVERLAY (most LinkedIn posts):
Leave the [left third / right third / top quarter / bottom third] clear as negative space for text overlay. Place the primary visual subject in the [right two-thirds / center / left half].

For PORTRAIT-focused images:
Eye-level camera, subject occupying center-left third, strong rule-of-thirds composition, subject looking slightly camera-left or directly at camera, environmental context visible in background right third.

For CONCEPT / DATA images (no people):
Centered symmetrical composition with the primary graphic element at optical center, generous negative space in the upper 30% and lower 20%, strong visual hierarchy leading the eye from top to bottom.

For CAROUSEL slides:
Consistent visual grammar: identical margin/padding proportions, same color zones as previous slides, visual element anchored to bottom-left or top-right, progression implied by color shift or scale change.

For WIDE LANDSCAPE posts (1.91:1):
Widescreen cinematic composition, subject positioned at left-third intersection point, background landscape fills the right two-thirds, strong depth of field differential.

## ANTI-PATTERNS - WHAT TO AVOID

Stock photo cliches (credibility killers for B2B):
handshake photo, people pointing at whiteboard, staged group fist-bump, person in suit smiling too broadly, puzzle pieces connecting, light bulb floating above head, generic boardroom with laptops, diverse group laughing at laptop for no reason

Technical quality issues:
blurry, watermark, text artifacts, distorted faces, deformed hands, extra fingers, AI uncanny valley, oversaturated, low resolution, pixelated, JPG compression artifacts

Off-brand for most B2B contexts:
cartoonish proportions, childlike illustration, heavy drop shadows, overly decorated borders, skeuomorphic textures, gradient mesh from the early 2000s, neon on white, rainbow effects
